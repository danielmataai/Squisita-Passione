Discover Italy Through Its Food

Join me on an unforgettable journey through Italy's most authentic culinary destinations. These aren't typical tourist experiences—they're informal, personal adventures where you'll meet my friends, visit hidden gems, and taste flavors that most travelers never discover.

Based around Florence and Tuscany, each tour is carefully curated to give you an insider's perspective on Italian food culture. From family-run trattorias to local markets, artisan producers to historic vineyards, you'll experience the real Italy.

Sample Itinerary

Every tour is customized, but here's an example of what a day might look like:

AM

Morning Market Visit

Start the day at a local market, discovering seasonal produce, artisan cheeses, and fresh bread. Learn how to select the best ingredients like a local.

Mid

Vineyard Experience

Visit a family-owned vineyard for a private tour and tasting. Meet the winemaker and learn about traditional Tuscan wine production.

PM

Lunch at a Hidden Trattoria

Enjoy a long, leisurely lunch at a restaurant known only to locals. Multiple courses of authentic regional dishes paired with local wines.

Eve

Artisan Workshop

End the day with a visit to an artisan—perhaps a cheese maker, olive oil producer, or pasta craftsman—for a hands-on experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

Tours can range from single-day experiences to week-long adventures. Most guests choose 3-5 days to fully immerse themselves in the experience. We'll customize the duration based on your interests and schedule.

Tours typically include all planned meals, tastings, activities, and my services as your guide. Transportation within Italy, accommodation, and flights are usually arranged separately, though I'm happy to provide recommendations.

To maintain the intimate, personal nature of the experience, I keep groups small—typically 2-8 people. Private tours for couples or families are also available.

Tuscany is beautiful year-round. Spring (April-June) offers mild weather and blooming landscapes. Fall (September-November) brings harvest season and truffle hunting. Summer is warm and vibrant, while winter offers a quieter, more authentic local experience.
